The first thing you should do now is to contact your partner and arrange for a way to distribute the work. Many options are possible (one goes through everything first and the other checks, you divide arbitrarily and check the other's work quickly, etc.).
Important is that every submission should be looked at by both program committee members, though one may read it more completely than the other. Important: the web-based system does not check if you're both looking at the same record. It is therefore possible to change things without your partner noticing if (s)he is logged in at the same time (the webpage is only updated when you click on it yourself, not if somebody else makes changes). So please coordinate!

Practical instructions:

  1. Go to http://www.neuroinf.org and click 'my menu' to log on.
  2. Select the menu option: Review records in the database -> CNS 2002, paper submission form.
  3. You see now a list of all the submissions you need to review, one line for each submission.
  4. Press the button at the right side on the line to view the pdf file containing the submission. Use the click button to reject the paper if its subject is not suitable for the meeting (= not relevant to the function of real brains) or if it is very poorly written. If the author requested an oral presentation you will need to select two reviewers. To ensure an equal distribution of submissions over reviewers you should try to select every reviewer at least once and not more than three times (optimal is two) over all submissions you review. The expertise of reviewers is available here. The comment field can be used to communicate with your partner during this step of the review process, for example to flag a problem submission.
  5. When you are done: press submit to save the final changes.
